The Art of Layering Fragrances

By Emma Clarke • March 10, 2026 • 5 min read
Fragrance Guides

Photo: Minimalist fragrance collection

Layering fragrances is like composing a personal symphony—each note contributes to a unique olfactory experience. When done right, it creates a signature scent that lingers beautifully throughout the day.

Why Layer?

Layering allows you to customize your fragrance, making it more complex and personal. It can also extend the longevity of lighter scents and soften stronger ones. The key is balance.

The Basics: Notes & Families

Fragrances are built with top, middle, and base notes. When layering, start with a base scent (like vanilla or musk), then add a middle note (floral or spice), and finish with a fresh top note (citrus or green).

Stick to complementary fragrance families—for example, woody and citrus, or floral and gourmand. Avoid mixing too many strong notes that compete.

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Start clean: Apply unscented moisturizer to create a base.
  2. Layer from heaviest to lightest: Apply the strongest scent first (usually base notes), then lighter ones on top.
  3. Focus on pulse points: Wrists, neck, behind ears—areas where warmth helps diffuse the scent.
  4. Test and adjust: Try combinations on skin before committing; body chemistry affects the final aroma.

Our Favorite Combinations

  • Vanilla + Sandalwood: Warm, creamy, and grounding.
  • Rose + Oud: Romantic with an exotic edge.
  • Bergamot + Musk: Fresh yet sensual.
  • Jasmine + Amber: Floral and rich.
